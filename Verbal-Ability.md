# Verbal Ability — Tips, Strategies & Key Concepts

## ✍️ Sentence Completion

### Strategy
1. **Read the full sentence first** — understand the context
2. **Look for clue words** — however, but, although, therefore, moreover
3. **Positive/negative tone** — match the blank to the overall tone
4. **Eliminate wrong options** — grammar check each choice

### Common Patterns
- **Contrast**: although...yet, despite...nevertheless
- **Cause-Effect**: because, therefore, hence, consequently
- **Addition**: moreover, furthermore, in addition
- **Example**: for instance, such as, namely

### Tips
- Fill in the blank mentally BEFORE looking at options
- The correct answer will sound natural when read aloud
- Watch for parallel structure (matching verb forms, tenses)

## 🔍 Error Identification

### Common Error Types

1. **Subject-Verb Agreement**
   - Singular subject → singular verb
   - Collective nouns (team, group) → singular verb
   - "Each" and "Every" → singular verb
   - "Neither...nor" → verb agrees with NEARER subject

2. **Tense Errors**
   - Past → past throughout (don't mix)
   - Present perfect → have/has + past participle
   - Past perfect → had + past participle (for earlier of two past events)

3. **Pronoun Errors**
   - Pronoun must agree with antecedent in number and gender
   - "Who" for subjects, "Whom" for objects
   - "Its" = possession, "It's" = it is

4. **Article Errors**
   - "A" before consonant sounds, "An" before vowel sounds
   - "The" for specific/unique things
   - No article with uncountable nouns used generally

5. **Preposition Errors**
   - "In" for months/years/long periods
   - "On" for days/dates
   - "At" for specific times
   - "Between" for two, "Among" for more than two

### Quick Check
- Read each sentence segment independently
- If any segment sounds wrong in isolation, it has an error

## 📖 Reading Comprehension

### Speed Reading Strategy
1. **Read questions FIRST** — know what to look for
2. **Skim the passage** — focus on first/last sentences of each paragraph
3. **Identify the main idea** — usually in the first paragraph
4. **Note key details** — names, dates, numbers, transitions

### Question Types
- **Main Idea**: "What is the passage mainly about?"
- **Inference**: "It can be inferred that..."
- **Detail**: "According to the passage..."
- **Vocabulary in Context**: "The word X in line 3 means..."

### Tips
- Main idea = broadest answer choice
- Inference = answer supported by passage (not stated directly)
- Detail = find exact line in passage
- Don't use outside knowledge — stick to the passage

### Time Management
- Spend 2-3 min reading passage
- Spend 30 sec per question
- If stuck, eliminate obviously wrong options and guess

## 🔀 Passage Ordering / Sentence Rearrangement

### Strategy
1. **Find the opening sentence** — introduces the topic, no pronouns referring to previous
2. **Identify pronoun references** — "this," "they," "it" refer to something before
3. **Look for chronological markers** — first, then, finally, later
4. **Find the concluding sentence** — summarizes or gives opinion

### Tips
- Sentences with proper nouns often come first
- Sentences starting with "However," "But," "Nevertheless" are NEVER first
- "In conclusion," "To sum up" signals the last sentence

## 🎯 General Verbal Tips
1. **Read daily** — newspaper editorials improve comprehension speed
2. **Learn 10 new words daily** — use them in sentences
3. **Practice with timer** — 25 mins for 25 questions
4. **First instinct is usually right** — don't overthink
5. **Attempt all questions** — no negative marking
