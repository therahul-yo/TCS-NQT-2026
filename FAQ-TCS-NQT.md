# TCS NQT — Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## 📋 General Questions

### Q: What is TCS NQT?
**A:** TCS National Qualifier Test — an online exam by Tata Consultancy Services to hire freshers. It's a multi-level assessment that categorizes candidates into Ninja, Digital, or Prime roles based on performance.

### Q: Who can apply?
**A:**
- B.Tech / B.E / M.E / M.Tech / MCA / M.Sc students
- 2025/2026 batch
- Generally 60%+ in 10th, 12th, and graduation
- No active backlogs (some flexibility varies)

### Q: Is TCS NQT free?
**A:** The basic NQT is free. Some variants (NQT Cognitive, NQT IT) may have a fee (~₹500-700). Check the official TCS iON website.

### Q: How many times can I take TCS NQT?
**A:** You can take it once per hiring cycle. Your best score is considered.

## 📝 Exam Questions

### Q: What is the exam pattern?
**A:**
- **Part A (Foundation)**: 65 questions, 75 minutes
  - Numerical Ability (20 Q, 25 min)
  - Verbal Ability (25 Q, 25 min)
  - Reasoning Ability (20 Q, 25 min)
- **Part B (Advanced)**: 15 MCQs + 2 Coding, 115 minutes
  - Advanced Quant + Reasoning (15 Q, 25 min shared)
  - Coding (2 Q, 90 min)

### Q: Is there negative marking?
**A:** **NO!** There is NO negative marking. Always attempt every question.

### Q: Is the exam adaptive?
**A:** No, TCS NQT is **non-adaptive** this year. All candidates get the same difficulty level.

### Q: Can I use a calculator?
**A:** A calculator will be available on your screen. No physical calculator allowed.

### Q: Can I use rough paper?
**A:** Rough paper is available **on your screen** (digital). No physical rough paper allowed.

### Q: What languages can I use for coding?
**A:** C, C++, Java, Python. Python is recommended for beginners (shorter code).

### Q: Can I look away from the screen?
**A:** **NO!** The exam is proctored. Looking away can flag you for cheating. Keep your eyes on the screen.

### Q: What if my internet disconnects?
**A:** Your progress is saved. You can re-login and continue where you left off. But don't take the risk — ensure stable internet.

## 🎯 Scoring & Cutoffs

### Q: What are the different profiles?
**A:**
| Profile | Package | What You Need |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Ninja | ₹3.36 LPA | Clear Foundation + basic Advanced |
| Digital | ₹7 LPA | Good Advanced + Coding scores |
| Prime | ₹12 LPA | Excellent Advanced + Coding |

### Q: What is the cutoff?
**A:** Cutoffs vary by year and company needs, but generally:
- Foundation: 50-55%
- Ninja: 55-60% overall
- Digital: 70-75% overall
- Prime: 85%+ overall

### Q: How is the score calculated?
**A:**
- Foundation section score
- Advanced section score
- Coding section score
- Overall composite score determines your profile

### Q: Can I get Digital/Prime without coding?
**A:** Very unlikely. Coding section is critical for Digital and Prime profiles. Even solving 1 problem well can boost your profile.

## 📅 Registration & Schedule

### Q: How to register?
**A:**
1. Go to [TCS iON](https://www.tcsion.com/)
2. Create an account
3. Search for TCS NQT
4. Fill the form and pay (if applicable)
5. Select your preferred date and center

### Q: When is the exam?
**A:** TCS NQT is conducted multiple times a year. Check the TCS iON portal for upcoming dates. Your exam is **April 3, 2026**.

### Q: Can I choose my exam date?
**A:** Yes, during registration you can select from available dates and centers.

### Q: Where is the exam conducted?
**A:** At designated TCS iON centers or online (depends on the variant).

## 💻 Technical Questions

### Q: Which coding language should I choose?
**A:**
- **Python**: Best for beginners, shortest code, easy syntax
- **Java**: Most commonly used, good for TCS developers
- **C/C++**: Fastest execution, but more complex
- **Recommendation**: Python if you're new, Java if you know it

### Q: How hard is the coding section?
**A:**
- Q1: Easy to Medium (arrays, strings, basic logic)
- Q2: Medium (sorting, searching, basic DP)
- Difficulty: Less than LeetCode Medium, more than Easy

### Q: Can I use built-in functions?
**A:** Yes! Use `sort()`, `max()`, `min()`, `Counter`, etc. They're allowed.

### Q: How is the code judged?
**A:**
- Multiple test cases (hidden)
- Your code must pass ALL test cases to get full marks
- Partial marks for passing some test cases
- Time limit: usually 1-2 seconds per test case

## 🏢 After the Exam

### Q: What happens after clearing NQT?
**A:**
1. You get interview calls from TCS
2. Technical interview (DSA, projects, coding)
3. HR interview
4. Offer letter

### Q: How long is the NQT score valid?
**A:** Usually 1-2 years. TCS uses your score for hiring during this period.

### Q: Can other companies see my NQT score?
**A:** No, NQT is specific to TCS. But some other companies also use TCS iON for hiring.

### Q: What if I don't clear NQT?
**A:** You can appear again in the next hiring cycle. Use the time to prepare better.

## 🎒 Exam Day Tips

### Q: What to carry to the exam center?
**A:**
- Government-issued photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)
- Registration confirmation printout
- Nothing else (no phones, watches, calculators)

### Q: What if I'm taking it online?
**A:**
- Stable internet connection
- Working webcam and microphone
- Quiet room with no one else
- Clear desk (nothing on it)
- Desktop/Laptop (no mobile)

### Q: What if I face technical issues?
**A:** Contact TCS iON support immediately. Keep the helpline number handy.

## ⏰ Time Management

### Q: How much time for each section?
**A:**
- Numerical: 25 min (1.25 min/question)
- Verbal: 25 min (1 min/question)
- Reasoning: 25 min (1.25 min/question)
- Advanced MCQs: 25 min (1.5 min/question)
- Coding: 90 min (45 min/question)

### Q: What if I run out of time?
**A:** 
- No negative marking — guess the remaining questions
- Partial code submissions get partial marks
- Don't leave anything blank!

## 🆘 Common Problems

### Q: I'm weak in coding. Can I still get Ninja?
**A:** Yes! Ninja mainly requires clearing the Foundation section. Focus on aptitude, and try to at least attempt the coding questions.

### Q: I'm weak in aptitude. Can I still get Digital?
**A:** Difficult. You need to clear Foundation first. Focus on the high-weightage topics: Percentages, Time & Work, Number Series.

### Q: Can I switch between sections?
**A:** Within Foundation, sections are usually sequential. Within Advanced, you can switch between MCQs and Coding.

### Q: Is there a sectional cutoff?
**A:** Generally, you need to clear the Foundation section overall. Individual sectional cutoffs vary by year.
